The Story

A Life Built on Truth

Olusegun Ariyo is a Nigerian journalist, urban planner, writer, and social justice activist whose career spans some of Nigeria's most respected media institutions. His voice carries the weight of decades in the field — not as a mere observer, but as an active participant in shaping the nation's urban and civic discourse.

He is the founder, publisher and editor of Urban Express News — a platform built to give voice to the stories the mainstream often overlooks. As a certified urban planner, he brings a rare analytical lens to media commentary on Nigeria's cities, infrastructure challenges, land rights, and community development.

His book, "Solution to Processing Land & Property Documents in Lagos State", stands as one of the most accessible and authoritative guides for Nigerians navigating the complex terrain of property ownership — a testament to his commitment to democratizing vital civic knowledge.

"Every story I tell is a brick in the wall of a more just, more planned, and more equitable Nigeria."

Three Disciplines

One Unified Mission

📰

The Journalist

Trained across Nigeria's finest newsrooms — ThisDay, PM News, Commoner Newspaper, and The Nation — Olusegun pursues unvarnished truth with every byline, from boardrooms to street corners.

🏙️

The Urban Planner

As a member of the Nigerian Institute of Town Planners, he applies systematic analytical thinking to Lagos' most complex challenges — housing, transportation, land rights, and community infrastructure.

⚖️

The Advocate

Social justice is not a pastime — it is a calling. From Okada rider rights to environmental advocacy, he fights for those whose voices are too often silenced by policy, poverty, and power.
