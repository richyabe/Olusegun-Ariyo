Advocacy & Impact

For Olusegun Ariyo, journalism is never neutral. Every story is an act of advocacy — for the displaced, the marginalized, the silenced, and the forgotten. These are the campaigns, the causes, and the communities he has dedicated his voice and platform to.

Through Urban Express News, years of field reporting, and sustained community organizing, Olusegun has built a track record of using media as a tool for genuine social transformation across Nigeria's urban centres.

Areas of Focus

Causes That Matter

These are not abstract issues — they are the lived realities of millions of Nigerians who deserve a journalist willing to speak truth on their behalf.

🛵

Okada Rider Rights & Urban Mobility

Championed the cause of commercial motorcyclists displaced by government bans in Lagos. Documented their economic collapse, families affected, and the unintended consequences of transport policy that ignored the urban poor.

Continues to push for inclusive mobility planning that respects all road users and provides viable economic alternatives for displaced transport workers.

Read coverage on Urban Express ↗
🏠

Housing Rights & Forced Evictions

Exposed systematic forced evictions of low-income communities in Lagos for real estate development. Reported on government demolitions carried out without legal notice, compensation, or provision of alternative shelter.

Holds authorities accountable through investigative journalism while building public awareness of constitutional protections available to affected residents.

Read coverage on Urban Express ↗
🌊

Environmental & Climate Justice

Documented the destruction of Lagos wetlands and coastal communities through irresponsible development. Advocates for mandatory environmental impact assessments before large construction projects receive approval.

Amplifies the voices of communities facing climate-induced displacement, flood vulnerability, and environmental degradation across Lagos State and coastal Nigeria.

Read coverage on Urban Express ↗
📋

Land & Property Rights Education

Through his landmark book and ongoing journalism, Olusegun has democratized access to information about property rights in Lagos State — empowering women, the poor, and first-time buyers to navigate complex land documentation systems.

Works to dismantle the informational gatekeeping that enables corruption and exploitation in Nigeria's property sector.

Women's Rights & Economic Inclusion

Investigates and campaigns against gender discrimination in property ownership, inheritance laws, and market access. Amplifies the economic agency of Nigerian women through targeted journalism and civic education.

Partners with civil society organizations to translate media coverage into policy reform at state and federal levels.

Read coverage on Urban Express ↗
🏪

Informal Sector & Street Trader Rights

Champions the rights of market traders, street vendors, and informal workers facing harassment, market demolitions, and exclusion from urban development planning processes.

Believes the informal economy is not a problem to be solved, but a vital economic infrastructure that deserves recognition, protection, and integration into city planning.

Read coverage on Urban Express ↗

The Advocacy Philosophy

"I believe journalism has a responsibility that goes beyond informing — it must transform. When I report on a community facing eviction, I'm not just documenting a story; I am building a record of injustice that the powerful cannot easily erase."

— Olusegun Ariyo, Publisher, Urban Express News
